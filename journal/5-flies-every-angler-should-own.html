The Essential Five

Streamlines your fly box can be one of the best ways to improve your fishing. Many anglers carry hundreds of flies, yet only fish a dozen. Most of the year, trout are focused on a handful of patterns that cover most insect profiles.

After years on the water, we've narrowed it down to five "can't-miss" patterns that every angler should own. These aren't just for experts — they are the bread and butter of any successful trip.

  • The Zebra Midge: Covers technical tailwater and winter fishing. (#18–22)
  • The Pheasant Tail: Matches almost any mayfly nymph profile. (#14–18)
  • The Elk Hair Caddis: High-floating and skitterable during evening rises. (#14–18)
  • The Parachute Adams: The ultimate general dry fly for mayfly hatches. (#16–20)
  • The Wooly Bugger: Versatile streamer that mimics everything from leeches to baitfish. (#8–10)

Each of these patterns addresses a specific insect type and life stage. Together, they form a "Swiss Army knife" approach to fly fishing.
