Mastering Midge Presentations

Midges are the most abundant insects in almost any trout stream, and on tailwaters, they are often the only show in town for much of the winter and early spring. However, despite their numbers, they can be incredibly frustrating to fish successfully.

The key to successful midge fishing lies in three technical adjustments: depth, dead-drift accuracy, and tippet size.

1. Perfect Depth

Midges spend most of their life as larvae on the bottom or as pupae slowly ascending through the water column. If you aren't within 6 inches of the bottom, you're missing 90% of the fish. Use small split shot and a technical indicator to find that sweet spot.

Recommended Setup

9ft 6x Tapered leader with 18" of 6x or 7x fluorocarbon tippet. Single Zebra Midge #20.

Fish usually congregate in the slower moving "technical" water when midges are active. Look for the seams where fast water meets slow water — that's where the bugs are concentrate.
