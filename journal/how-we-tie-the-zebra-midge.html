Tying for Results

At Arrowhead, we don't just tie for the sake of tying — we tie for results. Our flies are carefully selected and built using high-carbon hooks and weighted for depth control. But why does the Zebra Midge in particular work so well?

It's all about the distribution of the copper rib and the delicate taper. We've spent countless hours on the bench refining these details to ensure they produce in technical water.

The Taper Matters

The Zebra Midge is a simple pattern, but there are three things we focus on during every tie:

  1. The Tapered Thread Body: A thin, smooth taper towards the eye of the hook creates a realistic insect profile.
  2. The Tungsten Bead: Each bead is matched to the hook size for the fastest sink rates, avoiding too much bulk.
  3. Counter-Ribbed Wire: Gives the fly durability and adds subtle segmentation that catches the light like a natural.

Hand-Tied to Order

Each fly is tied on-site at our workshop with attention to these critical details. We test these on our own home waters to ensure they work for you.
